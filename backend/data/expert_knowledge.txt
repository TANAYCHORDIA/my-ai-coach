### INJURY: Shoulder Strain (Common in bowlers and fielders)
Fact 1: Overuse of the rotator cuff muscles leads to shoulder strain in frequent throwers and bowlers.  
Fact 2: Avoid heavy overhead lifts during pain; focus on internal and external rotation strengthening.  
Fact 3: Safe exercises: resistance band pull-aparts, face pulls, and scapular push-ups.  
Fact 4: Warm-ups should include shoulder circles and band stretches before every bowling session.  
Fact 5: Ice therapy and proper rest between bowling spells help prevent chronic injury.  
Fact 6: Gradually increase bowling volume — sudden workload spikes increase risk by over 50%.  
Fact 7: Emphasize posture correction (avoid forward shoulder slump) to maintain shoulder alignment.  

### INJURY: Lower Back Pain (Common in fast bowlers and batsmen)
Fact 1: Repetitive spinal rotation during bowling causes lumbar strain.  
Fact 2: Avoid heavy squats or deadlifts during recovery; substitute with glute bridges and bird-dogs.  
Fact 3: Core stabilization through planks and side planks improves lumbar endurance.  
Fact 4: Use proper bowling action with hip rotation instead of pure back extension.  
Fact 5: Include hamstring and hip flexor stretches post-match to relieve spinal tension.  
Fact 6: Swimming and yoga are excellent low-impact recovery activities.  
Fact 7: Avoid long sitting periods immediately after matches — walk or stretch instead.
